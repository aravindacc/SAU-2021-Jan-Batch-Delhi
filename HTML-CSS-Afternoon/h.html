Tesla in India

Electric car maker Tesla to route its India Investment through the jurisdiction of the Netherlands, which is tax-friendly, in an exception to the general trend. Tesla Motors Amsterdam is the parent company for Tesla Motors and Energy, India, the Economic Times mentioned in a report citing company’s incorporation documents. This corporate structure in India would offer Tesla tax benefits related to capital gains and dividend payments, say experts.Other global auto makers such as MG Motors, which entered India in 2017 invested via China, which is also the home jurisdiction for its parent company SAIC Motors. Similarly, India investments of KIA Motors came from South Korea, which is also the corporate home of KIA Corp. However, Tesla decided not to invest through its home jurisdiction.

India records lowest fatalities in 8 months

India Covid-19 Live updates: India reported 13,788 new covid-19 cases in the last 24 hours. This is the first time this number has dropped below 15,000 mid-June last year. Out of the total cases, active cases further dropped to 2.08 lakh while recoveries went up to 1,02 crore. With 145 deaths reported on Sunday, the toll stands at over 1.52 lakh. Kerala recorded 5,005 new cases, more than 36 per cent of all. Maharashtra reported 3,081 cases. In a first, active cases declined below 2 per cent of the total cases reported in the country on Sunday. Daily new Covid-19 cases have stayed below the 20,000 mark from the last 10 days.

'Super Earth': NASA mission discovers 10 billion-year-old exoplanet 50% larger than our own NASA's TESS mission (Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite) has discovered a hot and rocky planet that is about 50 per cent larger than Earth and has three times the mass but surprisingly, the same density as Earth. Referred to as an exoplanet owing to its location outside our solar system, it has been named TOI-561b. The discovery of this exoplanet was revealed in a study accepted for publication in The Astronomical Journal. This study was made public during the 237th meeting of the American Astronomical Society, which was held virtually owing to the Covid-19 pandemic.

Indian, French Rafales to be part of bilateral air exercise 'Desert Knight 21' in Jodhpur

The Indian and French Rafales will be in action as part of 'war games' or bilateral air exercises in Jodhpur between January 20 and 24. This is the first time the newly inducted Rafales in the Indian Air Force will participate with the French Air Force Rafales. “Both the air forces will be operating with the Rafale, wargaming various scenarios," officials said.

Indian Air Force is hosting the French Air and Space Force (Armée de l’Air et de I’Espace) at Air Force Station Jodhpur for Exercise Desert Knight 21, a bilateral Air exercise from 20 to 24 Jan 21, officials said. The French forces are currently deployed in Asia as part of their ‘Skyros Deployment’, and will transit through India participating in the joint exercise. The aim of the exercise is to provide operational exposure and share best practices towards enhancing combat capability.
